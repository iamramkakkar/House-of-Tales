CREATIVITY IS A CHANGE

Creative thinking inspires ideas. Ideas inspires change.

Creativity remains a contentious and ill-defined concept, with a long history of propositions and contestations.

At best, there is a broad consensus that creativity involves a human process that leads to an innovative product. Much of the contention about defining the creative process results from the central place given to a significant event – the so-called “creative leap”, a sudden insight that is almost immediately recognized as the key to creating something new.

The problem is that for the most part, the recognition is necessarily retrospective and consequently unreliable: even the creator who is aware that a creative act has occurred may be unable to articulate how (or why) it occurred.

Understanding the peaks and lows that define a creative process, then using it to sustain energy and flow to support successful creative outcomes.

Creative inspiration is notoriously elusive. Being able to train creativity or induce a state of creativity has therefore long been the aim of many artists and scientists.

I think in this life it is important to be kind, be thankful and always be creative.
